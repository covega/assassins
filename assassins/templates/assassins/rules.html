Rules

  • Eliminate your targets and avoid being eliminated. The last assassin remaining wins.
  • Your weapon will be provided for you.
  • A successful assassination must go unwitnessed. Your shot cannot be witnessed by anyone (except the victim), or it is invalid.
  • An assassination is "witnessed" if any person sees the killer in the action, sees the gun, or sees the stream of water and can testify on the victim's behalf.
  • You have 72 hours to eliminate your target. If you fail, you will lose.
  • You have 2 hours after completing a kill to submit your assassination, or you will also be eliminated for stalling. You can trust your victim will enforce this.
  • An assassination may not occur while the victim is in a restroom, in an in-session class, at work (i.e. a job), in a place of worship, in the victim's dorm room, or in your dorm's dining hall (including eating areas).
  • A person is considered inside a room if any part of their body is inside the doorway
  • You may not tell anyone your target.
  • You may not warn your target if you are eliminated.
  • Targets may be reassigned without warning.
  • If your target times out, you will be notified and will receive a new target. Your time will not be extended.
  • The final two assassins will have their timers immediately reset. Each will have 48 hours to carry out their assassinations. After 48 hours, they will enter Sudden Death:
    • The living may carry out kills without concern for witnesses. A kill may occur in plain sight.
    • The fallen will be reinstated as terminators. They may kill either living player. Terminators may also kill in plain sight. Only the fallen may act as terminators.
    • Dorm rooms and dining halls will no longer be safe. Any living player or terminator may carry out a kill in any dorm room or dining hall. In-session classes, bathrooms, places of worship, and a playerâ€™s place of work are still safe.
  • Participate with honor. If you submit an assassination, it is assumed your target does not dispute it. Similarly, accept a fair assassination with honor.
  • If there is reasonable doubt about the validity of an assassination, it is considered invalid.
  • The game coordinator reserves the right to arbitrarily resolve all disputes and to eliminate anyone for violating the Assassins' Code of Honor.
  • Good luck.
